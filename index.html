Concept of Full Channel Assimilation

Operational numerical weather prediction centers have a long history of using multi-spectral and hyper-spectral infrared (IR) satellite observation to imporve their weather prediction skills. It is undisputed that impressive progress has been made regardless of the methodological inefficiency brought by channel selection approach in their data assimilation system treating hyper-spectral infrared observation. Hyper-spectral sensor observations's accuracy can be better than 0.2K if its spectral-resolving power (R = λ/∆λ) is higher than 1000, this means information content of hyper-spectral IR observation is higher than that of multi-spectral IR observation. However, a portion of information is deliberately discarded by channel selection and thus cannot make a difference to NWP. The figure below shows how full-channel assimilation of Cross-track Infrared Sounder (CrIS) observation could improve the accuracy of initial conidition in terms of Mean Absoulte Bias (MAB) and Standard Deviation (StDev) compared to assimilating the a few channel observations which are used byNational Centers for Environmental Prediction (NCEP) in its global forecast system. This result is produced by our Heimdallr 1.0 Data Assimilation System

Heimdallr 1.0 Data Assimilation System

The Heimdallr 1.0 data assimilation system is a 3-Dimensional hyper-spectral IR data assimilation system, which could handle randiance PC score, Full- and Selected-Channel radiance assimilation. With limited computational resource requirement and high availability, this system can be installed personal computers, single board computers, as well as HPC clusters using OpenMP and MPI with x86 and ARM instruction set.
